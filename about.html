Ounce began with a scale and a suspicion. We kept noticing that the garments we reached for — the ones that outlived every trend — all had one thing in common. They were heavy. Denser cloth, more of it, held together better. So we made weight the whole point.

Every piece we release is weighed and its mass printed on the label, in grams. It's a receipt for substance: proof that when you pay for an ounce hoodie, you're paying for cloth, not marketing. We don't do logos, seasons, or noise. We do a small number of blank, considered things and make them as well as we can afford to.

They're cut in short runs by makers we can call by name — mills in Japan, knitters in Scotland, a small factory outside Lisbon. Sold straight to you so the money goes into the cloth. And when yours finally wears through, write to us; we'd rather help you mend it than sell you another.
